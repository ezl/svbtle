Chicago Coffee Shops: Where I Work

If you’re working on a startup or freelancing, you often find yourself frequenting cafes around town. If you’re like me, you try to stay away from Starbucks.

I’ve compiled a list of personal favorites and recommendations from other startup friends, grouped by approximate geographic location.

If you’re funemployed like me, have other great spots I can add to the list, or want to join the Chicago Daytime Coffeeshop Circuit, or “CDCC” as I like to call it (yep, I just made that up), drop a line: ipoopdaily@rocketlease.com

Near North

  • Public Hotel - Hotel lobby has a cafe in it with a huge work table. Work in luxurious surroundings.
  • LM Bistro – Hotel Felix bar - I generally come here in the afternoons if I want to booze while I work. Great bourbon selection. Wifi: Free but spotty.
  • Whole Foods North Ave - Awesome. Huge work area on the second floor. Great food downstairs and a coffee bar. Also 6.00 bourbon pours and lots of great beer. Wifi: 2 hour limit, then just refresh your browser cookies; Huge plus == free parking.
  • Panera @ Division/Sedgewick - Eh, it’s Panera. But you can park, so I come occasionally.
  • Starbucks Pipers Alley - Eh, its starbucks, but its big so its easy to work with others here. Wifi its often brutal.
  • ING Direct Cafe
  • Cafe Des Architects - Sofitel hotel restaurant - dead on weekdays and they’re happy to have you just sit in. Gorgeous sunlit room.
  • Delightful Pastries - Lincoln park bakery, also dead on weekdays with nice staff. Tea isn’t cheap: $4.38. Pastries are cloyingly sweet. But 4.38 to rent an office for the afternoon works for me.
  • Elaines Coffee Call - Very cute spot in the Lincoln Hotel. Great almond milk latte.
  • Evas

Near But Not As Near North

  • Uncommon Ground - Only been to the Wrigleyville one. Nice big windows to let in the sun.
  • Next Door Cafe - Lets you book conference rooms for free, great restaurants nearby, and if you’re feeling adventurous, you can park at Trader Joe’s next door.
  • Floriole - Best pastries in the city. Wifi is usually good. Staff is generally nice, but J can get a little snarky if he thinks you’re sitting there too long.
  • The Bourgeois Pig - Classic spot, old two story house. Can get crowded.
  • Citygrounds - Typically a good hideaway that stays pretty quiet, even on weekends.
  • Bow Truss - Terrible spot to work, they’re really a roaster. No idea why I’m leaving this on the list. Very sexy website though.
  • Kickstand
  • Coffee and Tea

Even More North

  • Kopi Travelers - No Wifi, but i REALLY dig the atmosphere here. Get one of the spots by the window entrance its like a hippie Japanese tatami room.
  • Coffee Studio - Modern minimalist coffee shop with great espresso. Unforunately it often packs out.
  • Chava - I think this place was supposed to be a Starbucks, but then corporate backed out. Chava got a great, built out spot.
  • Book Cellar
  • Asado
  • Dollop
  • Julius Meinl - Packs out. Nice pastries

So Far North It Might As Well Be Wisconsin

  • Metropolis - Too far away, but if you’re in this hood, a good work spot.

Near West

  • Sip - Personal favorite. This is in my top 3. Cheap tea, cool staff, awesome vibe. Park on Hubbard.
  • Swim Cafe - Big open room can get cold and loud. Good food though. Can typically find parking in front of the school next door.
  • Big Shoulders Coffee
  • Lovely - Kind of a weird spot, its typically second place if swim is full.
  • Letizia’s Bakery
  • Jupiter Outpost

Old HipsterVille South (Wicker/Ukranian Village-ish)

  • Atomix
  • Cafe Ballou
  • Knockbox
  • Alliance Bakery
  • Prasino - Don’t go on weekends, you’ll be stepping on their lunch service, but during the week its wide open. A little smug, but I love the big windows.
  • Caffe Streets - I think this place gets too loud, but my friends dig it.

Old Hipsterville North (Bucktown)

  • Wormhole - They have an old school Nintendo. That is all that matters.
  • Filter - Spotty. Go to Wormhole.
  • Caffe Deluca - Not really the best work spot, but you can camp out for 2 hours or so and get great food.
  • Red June - Great all day work spot, but not the “warmest” surroundings.
  • Bon Bon - Eclectic decor, free wifi, great Vietnamese sandwiches. Proprieter, Tim, is a nice dude.

South

  • Artists Cafe – Conveniently near MT’s house or I wouldn’t ever go.
  • Little Branch – Even more convenient so this is pretty much the only South Loop spot I hit.
  • Overflow – Weirdly barren.
  • Efebina’s – Decent spot if you’re close to Chinatown
  • Jumping Bean – Not that great, but the only thing I know of near Honky Tonk BBQ.
  • Bridgeport Coffee Company – Good spot, street parking, but not worth the trek for me.
